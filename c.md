5.3. FERA-based evaluation\
In order to analyze if RAMSES achieved its goals, and col-\
lect criticisms and suggestions from experts, the proposed RA was\
sent to different stakeholders who answered a well-defined ques-\
tionnaire. We used a checklist-based questionnaire named FERA\
(Framework for Evaluation of Reference Architectures) [18]. This\
checklist corresponds to a list of questions that guide reviewers\
in detecting problems in documents that describe the reference\
architecture. FERA is based on RAModel, which, as introduced in\
Section 4, proposes a large set of elements that can be used to de-\
scribe reference architectures. FERA is composed of 113 multiple\
choice questions whose answers vary from �fully satisfactory� to\
�totally unsatisfactory�, and fields to add general comments. When\
applied to the respondents, these questions are grouped into 4\
stages: the stage 1 covers overview information, viewpoints, mod-\
els, stakeholders, concerns, legal regulation, ISO 42010 [41] con-\
formance, design and development issues, domain specific issues,\
conformance with other artifacts, and quality attributes; the stage\
2 comprises specific questions to stakeholders (Architect, Domain\
Expert, Developer, Tester, Integrator, QA, and others); the stage 3\
presents the conclusion of the general analysis; finally, the stage\
4 covers hardware and software concerns (the FERA checklists are\
available at jesustp.com/ramses.html). The main objectives of us-\
ing FERA is to validate whether: (i) the RA is properly represented;\
